Fried Hokkien Prawn Noodle

炒福建蝦麵



Introduction

This is the most popular dish in Singapore where people would queue for 2 hours just for this
The dish comprises of shrimps, squids and pork and two kind of noodles.
Coupled with home-made chilli sauce and lime, it is never a sin to indulge in this dish.


Geography and Origin

This dish originates from Fujian.
Being close to the sea, the addition of seafood into the cuisine elavates the flavor
Do you know Lychee? Fujian produces the best Lychee.

Ingredients

The main ingredients are

  • 250 gm Thick Vermicelli
  • 400 gm Yellow Noodle
  • 1/2 kg Medium Size Prawns
  • 2 Big Squid (Sotong)
  • 200 gm Pork Belly
  • 50 cents Bean Sprouts (Tow Gay)
  • 2-3 tsp Garlic finely chopped
  • 50 cents Chives (Ku Chye)
  • 6 Lime
  • 300 gm Pork Fats
  • 5-6 Eggs (Big)
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Fish Sauce
  • 1 tsp Light Soy Sauce
  • 6 TBsp Pork Oil From frying the pork lard
  • 3 TBsp Oil
  • 700 ml Boiling Water to make the prawn stock
  • 300 ml Stock from Squid and Pork Belly after steaming


Recipe is retrieved from: Burning Kitchen

Allergen Information:

  1. Gluten

Cooking Methods


PREPARATION METHOD

  1. Soak the thick vermicelli in a basin of tap water while preparing the rest of the ingredients.
  2. Separate the heads of the prawns and devein the prawns (see my video demonstration). Next, wash the heads and prawn bodies separately and drain.
  3. Clean and wash the squid (see my video demonstration first) and drain dry.
  4. Use a knife to scrape the skin of the pork belly and then rub with coarse salt to exfoliate dead skin and dirt. Wash and drain.
  5. Remove the roots of the bean sprouts, then wash and drain.
  6. Remove the skin from the garlic and chop finely.
  7. Wash the chives and cut into 4cm lengths.
  8. Wash the pork fat and cut into small cubes.
  9. Cut the limes into half and set aside for later use.

COOKING METHOD

  1. In a wok, put in the pork fats and turn on heat to medium high. Stir fry the pork fats until golden brown and crispy to extract the oil.
  2. Transfer the crispy pork lard into a bowl. Pour out the oil into another bowl and set aside for later use.
  3. In the same wok, add 3 TBsp of cooking oil (not pork oil) and turn the heat to medium high. Add in the 1 tsp chopped garlic and fry till aromatic.
  4. Next, add in the prawn heads and fry to mix well with the garlic. Press the prawn heads to extract all the juices and essence.
  5. Add 700-800 ml of boiling water to the wok and let it simmer for 10-15 mins. You will see an orangey layer of oil surfacing. Turn off heat, transfer the prawn stock into a pot over a sieve and discard the prawn heads.
  6. Wash the wok, add water and bring it to a boil. Place the prawns in a deep plate and steam for about 4-5 mins. Remove the prawns and place them on a bowl.
  7. Do likewise for the squid, steam it for about 10 mins. Then remove and set aside.
  8. Steam the pork belly over high heat for about 15-20 mins. When cooked, remove and set aside.
  9. Pour the juices from the squid and pork belly into the prawn stock (the stock should total up to about 1 litre).
  10. Remove the shell from the prawns and set aside.
  11. Cut the squid into 0.5cm cross-section slices and the head and tentacles into strips, then set aside. Cut the pork belly into strips and set aside.
  12. In a wok, heat up 3 TBsp of pork oil and 2 TBsp of oil over high heat.
  13. Add in half of the yellow noodles and half of the vermicelli. Then stir fry without breaking anything.
  14. Add 1/2 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp of light soy sauce and 3 TBsp of prawn stock to the noodles. Mix well and fry for about 3-4 mins.
  15. Push the noodle to one side of the wok, then add in 2-3 TBsp of pork oil to the wok. Add in 2 TBsp of garlic and fry till aromatic.
  16. Next, crack 3 eggs over the garlic and break them up gently. Drizzle 1 tsp of fish sauce on the eggs and swirl around. (Note: Do not turn the egg over at this point).
  17. When the egg is slightly charred, flip the noodle mixture over to cover the egg. Add in half the bean sprouts and stir fry with the noodles.
  18. Add half the prawn stock to the noodle mixture, then cover the wok and let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the noodle mixture to absorb the stock.
  19. Add in half of the prawns, squid, pork belly slices and chives into the noodle and mix well.
  20. Lastly, taste to see if more light sauce needs to be added. Transfer the noodle mixture into 3 individual plates.
  21. Repeat the same procedure to fry the remaining half of the noodles.
  22. Best served hot with lime, sambal chilli and pork lard.
