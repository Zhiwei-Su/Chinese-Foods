Braised Pork Rice

滷肉飯



Introduction

Braised Pork Rice is a staple food in Taiwan, well known for its budget friendly price and spices used to braise the pork.
In fact, some Taiwanese wants to make Braised Pork Rice their national dish.


Geography and Origin

The specialty of braised pork rice is meat sauce and gravy, which are the key part of the production. The methods and characteristics of braised pork rice are different in Tainan, Taichung, and Taipei. There are many kinds of food and snacks in Taiwan, but the most famous one is braised pork rice. Braised pork rice, also known as Lu Rou rice, is one of Taiwanese snacks.



Ingredients

The main ingredients are

  • 100 grams of pork
  • 400 grams of pork filling
  • 7 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 5 fresh shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 or 2 eggs(depends on your preference)
  • Appropriate amount of oil
  • 10ml cooking wine
  • 15ml light soy sauce
  • 15ml braised soy sauce
  • 5g sugar
  • a little rapeseed

Recipe is translated from: Douguo

Allergen Information:

  1. Soy Sauce (Gluten)

Cooking Methods

Here are the methods:

  • White rice: choose your favorite method (steaming or boiling can be used).
  • Make butter onion crisps: several red onions, cut and peeled, and cut into thin rings. Sprinkle a little starch and mix well. Heat a wok over medium heat, add oil, half-fry and half-fry the shallot rings, remove the oil, and put it on a kitchen towel to absorb the oil.
  • Wash the pork belly and wipe it dry. Put it in the refrigerator for 20 minutes to cut into small pieces. Cut the pork belly into small pieces as much as possible, so that it tastes better.
  • Wash the old ginger and garlic, chop finely, heat the pan over medium heat, and saute with a little oil.
  • Put the diced pork belly in the pot and stir-fry slowly until slightly colored to force out excess oil. Add appropriate amount of soy sauce, 1 star anise, appropriate amount of Shaoxing wine, a little rock sugar, appropriate amount of five-spice powder, pepper, crispy shallots and warm water (the amount of water is your own control). Stir-fry on high heat and bring it to a boil, then transfer to a casserole with slow-fire brine.

Tips:

  • In order to eliminate greasy feeling, the stewed pork rice will usually be served with some green vegetables, for a light method, just blanch it. It can be placed next to the meat or alone.
  • Onions are very important. Red onions are best. It is better to put the meat on when the onion changes color and fry the aroma.
  • After adding the brine, cook more to allow the juice to penetrate into the meat.
